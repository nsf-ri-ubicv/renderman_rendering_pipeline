1) Mixed up categories.
This is a first nugget, about the result of mixing up categories. Shown are test accuracy performance statistics for a bunch of two-level (gridded gabor/random) models. There are three problems (the tenway problem, cars vs planes, and furniture (chairs and tables) vs. animals (dogs and cats and reptiles) vs. vehicles (cars and boats and planes).

The control task is the actual category, while the "mixed up" task is one where the 3dmodels in the category have been shuffled, e.g. cars vs planes becomes [one group of mixed cars and planes] vs [another random of cars and planes].

Results are further broken down by which invariances were used: All, meaning translation, scaling, and inplane and out-of-plane rotation; translation only; and inplane rotation only.
The key thing to notice is that rotation is much harder than translation; and that mixing up the categories has an effect. (I will write more text later.)
